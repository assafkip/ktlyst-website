# Social Reaction Gate (ENFORCED)

This rule fires when the founder shares someone else's content (post, article, thread, screenshot) and asks to comment, reply, quote-post, or DM referencing it.

**Scope:** Reactions to other people's content on any platform (LinkedIn, X, Reddit, Medium, etc.)
**Out of scope:** Original posts authored by the founder (those follow content-output.md and voice-enforcement.md)

## Before Drafting Any Reaction

1. **Extract the poster's claims.** Read the content and list the specific claims, opinions, or positions the poster is making. No interpretation, just what they said.
2. **Show the extracted claims to the founder.** Do not draft anything until the founder confirms or corrects the extraction. This prevents putting words in the poster's mouth.
3. **Draft the reaction** using the engagement playbook's style rules (insight/connector/question) and the founder voice skill.
4. **Self-check for KTLYST drift.** After drafting, verify the reaction doesn't accidentally pitch, position, or name-drop KTLYST unless the founder explicitly asked to. Reactions are about the poster's ideas, not yours.

## Role Clarity

| Source | Role in reactions |
|--------|-------------------|
| `canonical/engagement-playbook.md` | Style, tone, length, DM format |
| `canonical/talk-tracks.md` | Background knowledge only, never copy into reactions |
| `my-project/current-state.md` | Reference for drift check, never surface in reactions |
| Poster's actual content | Primary source, extracted claims drive the reaction |

## What Counts as a Reaction

- Comment on someone's post
- Reply to someone's comment or thread
- Quote-post / repost with commentary
- DM that references someone's public content
- Email response to a shared article or newsletter

## Red Flags (block and ask)

- Reaction contradicts the poster's actual claims (misread)
- Reaction pivots to KTLYST positioning without founder request
- Reaction uses insider knowledge the poster didn't share publicly
- Reaction exceeds 4 sentences (engagement playbook max is 3-4)
